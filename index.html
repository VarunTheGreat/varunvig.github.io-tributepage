Ernest Hemingway

An extraordinary American writer, novelist and short-story writer.

Below is a Timeline of Hemingway's Life:

  • 1899 - Ernest Hemingway is born in Oak Park, Illinois.
  • 1905 - Since birth, Hemingway was dressed as a girl by his mother. His mother finally dressed him in boys clothes.
  • 1913 - Enrolled into Oak Park and River Forest High School.
  • 1917 - Graduates from school, but forgoes college and instead, opts for a job as a Cubs reporter.
  • 1918 - Leaves his job as a Cubs reporter to volunteer with Red Cross as an ambulance driver during the first World War.
  • 1918 - In Italy, Hemingway was wounded in war. While in hospital, he fell in love with a nurse and they planned to get married.
  • 1919 - His fiance calls him to say she had fallen in love with an Italian officer. His heartbreak lead to the novel, "A Farewell to Arms".
  • 1920 - Hemingway moves to Toronto, Ontario to take a job as a reporter for the Toronto Star newspaper.
  • 1921 - As a foreign correspondent in Paris for the Toronto Star, he falls in with a circle of writers and artists including Gertrude Stein and Ezra Pound.
  • 1925 - Ernest Hemingway meets F. Scott Fitzgerald at the Dingo Bar in Paris, just two weeks after the publication of The Great Gatsby.
  • 1926 - Hemingway's first novel, The Sun Also Rises, is published. The novel is critically acclaimed and commercially successful.
  • 1928 - Ernest moves from Paris to Key West and lives there on and off through the 1950s and completes the majority of his life's writing at the house.
  • 1929 - A Farewell to Arms is published. The novel's success makes Hemingway financially independent.
  • 1932 - Hemingway goes to Spain to research bullfighting for Death in the Afternoon, his critically lauded nonfiction book on the subject.
  • 1933 - Pauline and Ernest travel to Kenya for a ten-week safari. Hemingway falls in love with the continent.
  • 1937 - Hemingway travels to Spain to report on Spanish Civil War for the North American Newspaper Alliance.
  • 1940 - He remarries the journalist Martha Gellhorn and settles in Finca Vigia, the Cuban estate where they will live, off and on, for twenty years.
  • 1940 - The United States enters World War II. Hemingway volunteers for the Navy, outfitting his fishing boat Pilar with guns to hunt for German submarines off the coast of Cuba.
  • 1952 - The novella The Old Man and the Sea is published in Life magazine.
  • 1953 - Ernest Hemingway is awarded the Pulitzer Prize for The Old Man and the Sea.
  • 1954 - Hemingway is awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature, but is unable to travel to Stockholm due to serious injuries sustained in two separate plane crashes and a bushfire accident earlier in the year.
  • 1961 - Hemingway leaves Cuba forever following the 1959 revolution in which his acquaintance Fidel Castro leads communist revolutionaries to power.
  • 1961 - Suffering from depression, alcoholism, and numerous physical ailments, Ernest Hemingway commits suicide in Ketchum, Idaho.
